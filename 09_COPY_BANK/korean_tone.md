# Korean Tone

Last updated: 2026-05-26

## Direction

Korean copy should be:

- natural
- younger
- direct
- warm
- trendy without sounding childish
- premium but not stiff
- confident without sounding arrogant
- human, not corporate

## Style Rules

- Prefer clean, conversational Korean over formal corporate wording.
- Keep English brand name `Korea Grand Tour` unless a specific Korean rendering is requested.
- Avoid overexplaining. Let the feeling stay clear and simple.
- Keep safety rules direct and unambiguous.
- For public content, avoid private participant details and unrevealed route details.

## Useful Tone Targets

- Excited but grounded.
- Selective but welcoming.
- Road-trip energy with real safety boundaries.
- Community first, cars as the bridge.

## Watchouts

- Do not make the event sound like illegal street racing.
- Do not make it sound too exclusive or arrogant.
- Do not translate English literally if it becomes stiff Korean.
- Do not invent Korean place names, dates, fees, or route details.
