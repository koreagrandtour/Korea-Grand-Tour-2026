# Participant Interview Criteria - Private Working Draft

Last updated: 2026-05-27

Local-only operating guide. Do not publish participant identities, answers, phone numbers, emails, plates, insurance details, payment notes, or private conversations.

## Purpose

The interview is not a corporate screening. It protects the Korea Grand Tour spirit: safe driving, good energy, respect for Korea, respect for other participants, and readiness for a long 4-day road event.

## Fit Criteria

Use these criteria as the working check:

- Safe attitude: understands this is not a race and accepts convoy/route discipline.
- Alcohol rule: accepts that alcohol/driving means immediate eviction.
- Road maturity: can handle long-distance driving without ego, pressure, or risky showing off.
- Event spirit: warm, social, curious, respectful, and willing to be part of the group.
- Korea-centered interest: interested in the roads, towns, food, culture/history, farms, and landscapes, not only the car content.
- Reliability: likely to pay on time, attend briefings, join Kakao when requested, and follow instructions.
- Car readiness: car is suitable for a 4-day inland tour and the driver accepts insurance responsibility.
- Content/privacy awareness: accepts the public line-up rule and understands people identity is not disclosed unless approved.

## Red Flags

- Treats the event like a race.
- Minimizes alcohol/driving rules.
- Pushes for unsafe driving, illegal speed, or risky content.
- Seems unreliable on payment, agreement, insurance, or communication.
- Disrespects other drivers, Korean roads, local places, hotels, restaurants, or staff.
- Wants private route details before the planned reveal in a way that creates safety or privacy risk.

## Interview Output

Record only the minimum useful status in the tracker:

- `Approved`
- `Maybe / needs follow-up`
- `Not a fit`

Keep detailed private notes local-only and only when necessary.
