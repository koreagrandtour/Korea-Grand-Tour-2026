Date: May 19, 2026

## References Checked

- RouteMapper: rally and expedition route planning, roadbook generation, waypoint exports, and route-as-product positioning. Source: https://routemapper.net/
- RacePoint: responsive event maps with route details, markers, stations, road closures, and attendee utility. Source: https://www.racepoint.ca/
- Amigomap: interactive event-map marketing with mobile-first maps, video flyovers, popups, custom icons, and route storytelling. Source: https://www.amigomap.com/
- CourseMapp: embedded route maps for race websites with sponsor markers, elevation profiles, and participant clarity. Source: https://coursemapp.com/
- Race Day Map: simple event-map creation flow: create route, add points of interest, embed. Source: https://racedaymap.com/
- Gumball 3000: lifestyle rally framing, application CTA, participant aspiration, route-led identity. Source: https://gumball3000.com/

## What Works

- The best route-led sites make the map a living product, not a static illustration.
- Strong event-map products pair route sequence with useful markers, distance, activity, and mobile readability.
- Premium rally sites sell identity and emotion first, then logistics.
- Repeated CTAs work best when the page has already shown proof: route, cars, people, and dates.
- Motion should explain the route: draw the path, move the vehicle marker, reveal the next stop, and update the content panel.

## Direction For KGT

- Keep the bright optimistic palette from the current Korea Grand Tour mood.
- Use the original page architecture: Home, About, Route, Rides, Join.
- Build the Route page around an animated Korea map with stop-by-stop city information.
- Replace generic visual dependency with original site-owned art: route hero, city poster, lineup visual, and registration visual.
- Keep the implementation static and paste-friendly so it can be adapted to Weebly.

## May 19 UX Pass

- Homepage first viewport must answer three questions immediately: what it is, when it happens, and why someone should join.
- Treat Phase One like a festival reveal: it is exciting because the first names are live, but the final line-up still has Phase Two and Phase Three coming.
- Use the latest KGT logo and raw car/participant photos for credibility. Avoid using registered-car graphics as regular car photos because the model names are embedded in the artwork.
- Make the route behave like premium product scrollytelling: sticky visual, continuous scroll progress, moving vehicle marker, and stop details that update without hard jumps.
